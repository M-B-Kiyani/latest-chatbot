# Voice Commands Reference Card

Quick reference for voice-based appointment booking commands.

## Booking Commands

### Start a Booking

```
"I'd like to book an appointment"
"Schedule a meeting"
"Book a consultation"
"I need to make an appointment"
"Can I schedule a meeting?"
```

### Reschedule an Appointment

```
"I need to reschedule my appointment"
"Can I change my appointment time?"
"Move my meeting to a different day"
"Reschedule my booking"
"Change my appointment"
```

### Cancel an Appointment

```
"I want to cancel my appointment"
"Cancel my booking"
"Delete my appointment"
"I need to cancel"
"Remove my appointment"
```

### Quick Booking (All Info at Once)

```
"Book a meeting for [date] at [time], my name is [name], email [email]"
"Schedule a 30-minute consultation for tomorrow at 2 PM, I'm John Smith, john@example.com"
"I want to book December 5th at 3 PM, Sarah Johnson, sarah@company.com"
```

### Check Availability

```
"What times are available tomorrow?"
"Show me available slots for next Monday"
"When can I book a meeting?"
"What's your availability this week?"
"Do you have any openings on Friday?"
```

### Specify Date

```
"Tomorrow"
"Next Monday"
"December 5th"
"The 15th"
"This Friday"
"Next week"
```

### Specify Time

```
"2 PM"
"10:30 AM"
"3 o'clock"

"Afternoon"
```

### Specify Duration

```
"15 minutes"
"30-minute meeting"
"45 minutes"
"One hour"
"A quick 15-minute call"
```

## Example Conversations

### Scenario 1: Step-by-Step Booking

```
You: "I'd like to book an appointment"
AI: "Great! To get started, what's your full name?"

You: "John Smith"
AI: "Perfect! What's your email address?"

You: "john.smith@example.com"
AI: "Thank you. Which date would you prefer?"

You: "Tomorrow"
AI: "What time works best for you?"

You: "2 PM"
AI: "Perfect! I've booked your appointment for tomorrow at 2:00 PM..."
```

### Scenario 2: Express Booking

```
You: "Book a meeting for December 5th at 3 PM, my name is Sarah Johnson, email sarah@company.com"
AI: "Let me confirm: I'm booking a meeting for December 5th at 3:00 PM for Sarah Johnson at sarah@company.com. Is that correct?"

You: "Yes"
AI: "Excellent! Your appointment is confirmed..."
```

### Scenario 3: Check Availability First

```
You: "What times are available tomorrow?"
AI: "On November 29th, we have available slots at: 9:00 AM, 10:30 AM, 1:00 PM, 2:30 PM, and 4:00 PM. Would you like to book one of these times?"

You: "I'll take 10:30 AM"
AI: "Great! To book your appointment at 10:30 AM tomorrow, I'll need your full name..."
```

### Scenario 4: Rescheduling an Appointment

```
You: "I need to reschedule my appointment"
AI: "I can help you reschedule your appointment. What's the email address you used when booking?"

You: "john@example.com"
AI: "I found your appointment on December 1st at 2:00 PM. What new date would you like to reschedule it to?"

You: "December 5th at 3 PM"
AI: "Perfect! I've rescheduled your appointment to December 5th at 3:00 PM. You'll receive an updated confirmation email."
```

### Scenario 5: Cancelling an Appointment

```
You: "Cancel my appointment"
AI: "I can help you cancel your appointment. What's the email address you used when booking?"

You: "sarah@company.com"
AI: "Are you sure you want to cancel your appointment on December 3rd at 10:00 AM? Please say 'yes' to confirm."

You: "Yes"
AI: "Your appointment has been cancelled successfully. You'll receive a cancellation confirmation email."
```

## Troubleshooting Voice Commands

### AI Didn't Understand?

Try rephrasing:

- Instead of: "Sometime next week"
- Say: "Next Monday at 2 PM"

### AI Asking for Info You Already Gave?

Repeat it clearly:

- "My email is john@example.com"
- Spell it if needed: "J-O-H-N at example dot com"

### Want to Change Something?

Be explicit:

- "Change the time to 3 PM"
- "Actually, make it tomorrow instead"
- "Can we do 30 minutes instead of 15?"
